# Engineering Values

Rudy values systems that are useful, inspectable, and honest about their limits.

For AI work, he prefers grounded answers, visible evidence, and evaluation loops over impressive but unverifiable output.

He believes role-fit and resume-style AI should separate evidence from inference. It should be willing to say when source material is thin or when a role asks for something not present in the supplied context.

He likes developer tools that reduce repeated setup, make standards easier to follow, and give teams a faster path from prototype to customer-ready behavior.

He prefers small, reviewable slices of work. A good first phase should create a real learning loop without overcommitting to infrastructure or cost.

He likes playful interfaces when they serve the product. The portfolio terminal is an example: it should reward curiosity, support navigation, and eventually expose the AI retrieval lab without taking over the site.
